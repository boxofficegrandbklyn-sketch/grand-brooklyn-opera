The Artists

The singers, conductors, and directors who bring the season to life on our stage.

The Grand Brooklyn Opera House is proud to present an exceptional company of artists for the 2026–2027 season. From internationally celebrated sopranos to emerging talents making their Brooklyn debuts, our roster represents the finest voices in opera today.

Conductors
🎼
Maestro Dario Rosetti
Principal Conductor
Widely regarded as the preeminent interpreter of French romantic opera, Maestro Rosetti has led productions at La Scala, the Vienna State Opera, and the Paris Opéra. He joins us for his third consecutive season as Principal Conductor.
🎻
Maestro Antonio De Luca
Guest Conductor
Naples-born and Milan-trained, De Luca brings extraordinary authority to the Italian repertoire. His 2024 recording of Verdi's Otello was named Recording of the Year by Gramophone magazine.
Principal Sopranos
🎤
Valentina Marchetti
Dramatic Soprano
Rome-born Marchetti made her Carnegie Hall debut at 28 and has since performed at every major house in Europe and North America. Her voice — described by The Guardian as a force of nature given human form — anchors our most demanding productions.
🎤
Isabelle Fontaine
Lyric Soprano
A graduate of the Paris Conservatoire, Fontaine has become the definitive interpreter of the French lyric repertoire for her generation. Her Brooklyn debut last season drew a standing ovation that lasted eleven minutes.
🎤
Clara Esposito
Coloratura Soprano
Making her Grand Brooklyn Opera House debut this season, Esposito arrives fresh from a celebrated run at the Met. Opera News called her voice a diamond — precise, brilliant, and utterly without flaw.

🎤
Marco Ferrante
Dramatic Tenor
Born in Naples, Ferrante has performed leading tenor roles at La Scala, Covent Garden, and the Metropolitan Opera. His partnership with Maestro Rosetti has produced some of the most acclaimed recordings of the decade.
🎤
Luca Castellano
Lyric Tenor
Castellano's warm, flexible tenor has made him one of the most sought-after voices in the Italian repertoire. He returns to Brooklyn for his fourth season, where he has become something of a local favorite.
🎤
Henri Beaumont

The Paris-born Beaumont brings gravitas and warmth to every role he inhabits. His voice — described as a cathedral in sound by Le Monde — makes him the anchor of our French productions this season.

Our Story

A legacy of artistry

For over a century, Brooklyn's most beloved cultural institution has welcomed audiences to experience the transformative power of opera.

Brooklyn's Home for Opera Since 1887

The Grand Brooklyn Opera House was founded in 1887 by a group of Brooklyn civic leaders who believed that world-class opera should not be confined to Manhattan. What began as an intimate hall seating 400 has grown into one of the most storied performance venues in the northeastern United States.

Over thirteen decades, our stage has welcomed legendary sopranos, celebrated conductors, and emerging artists alike. We have survived two world wars, the Great Depression, and multiple restorations — each time emerging stronger and more committed to our founding mission.

Today, The Grand Brooklyn Opera House presents a full season of mainstage productions, chamber concerts, youth programming, and community events — rooted always in the belief that great art belongs to everyone.

A Brief History

1887

The Founding

Local philanthropist Cornelius Aldrich commissions architect James F. Pearce to design a grand opera house for the people of Brooklyn.

1923

The Golden Era

The house reaches its peak pre-war prominence, selling out every main stage production for three consecutive seasons.

1961

Near Closure

A community fundraising campaign saves the house from permanent closure after decades of deferred maintenance.

1994

Full Restoration

A $22 million restoration returns the Grand Hall to its original splendor with modern acoustics and full accessibility.

2019

New Leadership

Artistic Director Margaux Fontaine takes the helm, bringing the house its highest attendance in forty years.

Parking
The Grand Brooklyn Opera House operates a private on-site parking lot at 241 Clinton Avenue, directly adjacent to the main entrance.

Lot hours: Opens 90 minutes before each performance. Closes 60 minutes after curtain.

Rate: $18 flat rate per performance evening. Attendants on duty at all times.

Additional street parking is available on Clinton Avenue and surrounding blocks at no charge after 6:00 PM.

Private Rentals

Host your event in historic grandeur

From intimate gatherings to grand celebrations, our spaces provide an unmatched backdrop for your most important moments.

The Grand Brooklyn Opera House offers exceptional event spaces for private and corporate hire. Each space carries the weight of history and the capability of a modern production facility.
